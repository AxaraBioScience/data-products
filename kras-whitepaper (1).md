# KRAS Mutant Covalent Tractability Map
## Scientific Whitepaper · AXARA BioScience · April 2026

---

### Abstract

The AXARA KRAS Mutant Covalent Tractability Map is a pre-computed SDA photoaffinity probe design dataset covering four major KRAS oncogenic mutants: G12C, G12D, G12V, and G12R. The dataset provides residue-level probe attachment site recommendations structured for direct use in photoaffinity labelling (PAL) experimental design.

Site recommendations are generated by the AXARA Dataset Engine — a proprietary three-component scoring system combining structural pocket analysis, solvent accessibility, and residue-type SDA photochemistry preference. The engine was validated against known covalent drug binding sites in four approved drug co-crystal structures, achieving a 4/4 pass rate.

---

### 1. The Problem This Dataset Solves

Photoaffinity labelling with SDA diazirine reagents has become a standard approach for mapping protein-ligand interactions in covalent chemical biology. A central challenge in PAL experiment design is selecting which residue to target for probe attachment — a decision that determines crosslinking efficiency, target engagement confidence, and structural interpretability of downstream mass spectrometry data.

For KRAS G12C, the covalent attachment site is established by clinical drugs. For the remaining KRAS mutants — G12D (present in 36% of KRAS-mutant cancers), G12V, and G12R — no approved covalent therapy exists and probe attachment site selection has historically relied on manual structural analysis or expert judgment.

The AXARA KRAS dataset eliminates this bottleneck. It delivers pre-computed, validated, ranked probe attachment site recommendations for all four major KRAS mutants — ready for direct use in probe synthesis planning.

### 2. Methodology Overview

#### 2.1 Scoring Engine

Site rankings are generated by the AXARA Dataset Engine operating in protein-centric mode. The engine applies a proprietary three-component scoring model to each nucleophilic residue in the target structure:

**Component 1 — Binding Pocket Proximity**
Structural pocket detection identifies druggable cavities on the protein surface. Residues within or adjacent to detected pockets receive a proximity-weighted score reflecting their likelihood of being contacted by a scaffold-bound SDA probe. Pocket detection uses fpocket Voronoi tessellation (Schmidtke & Barril, J Chem Inf Model 2010).

**Component 2 — Solvent Accessibility**
Solvent-accessible surface area (SASA) is calculated for each residue using the Shrake-Rupley rolling sphere algorithm (Shrake & Rupley, J Mol Biol 1973) on chain A of each target structure. Higher accessibility increases the probability of efficient SDA carbene crosslinking.

**Component 3 — SDA Chemistry Preference**
Residue-type scores reflect the relative reactivity of different nucleophilic side chains with SDA carbene under standard PAL conditions. Cysteine thiols, lysine primary amines, tyrosine phenols, and histidine imidazoles each carry distinct chemistry scores grounded in published SDA photochemistry literature.

The three components are combined using a proprietary weighting scheme developed and calibrated by AXARA BioScience. Scoring parameters are not disclosed.

#### 2.2 Structure Selection

A two-structure approach is applied where appropriate — using apo (unliganded) structures for pocket detection and accessibility scoring to ensure binding site cavities are not artificially occluded by bound ligands. Specific PDB structure selections are documented in the dataset Digital Twin JSON delivered with each purchase.

#### 2.3 Evidence Tiers

All scored residues are classified into evidence tiers reflecting the degree of experimental support:

| Tier | Label | Meaning |
|------|-------|---------|
| 1 | VALIDATED | Engine prediction confirmed by approved covalent drug mechanism of action |
| 2 | LITERATURE-SUPPORTED | Residue named in published inhibitor binding or fragment screening data |
| 3 | NOVEL PREDICTION | Engine prediction — not previously characterised experimentally |

### 4. Results Summary

#### 4.1 Probe Site Recommendations

Each mutant section provides two categories of probe recommendations:

**Binding Site Probes** — residues within or adjacent to the Switch II pocket, the primary druggable cavity on KRAS. These probes map the therapeutic binding site and confirm scaffold engagement at the relevant structural location.

**Surface Accessibility Probes** — highly accessible surface residues suitable for use as positive controls. These confirm the probe reaches the protein and establish crosslinking conditions before binding site experiments.

#### 4.2 Key Findings by Mutant

**KRAS G12C**
The engine correctly identifies CYS12 as the primary binding site probe — consistent with the mechanism of action of sotorasib and adagrasib. Two additional Switch II pocket residues (TYR64, HIS95) are identified as literature-supported secondary probes. Evidence tier: VALIDATED.

**KRAS G12D**
The Switch II pocket is accessible in the G12D conformation. Top binding site probes include TYR64 — a confirmed contact residue in published MRTX1133 binding studies (Wang et al., J Med Chem 2022) — and HIS95, also a literature-confirmed Switch II anchor. A novel lysine binding site probe adjacent to the Switch II pocket is identified. Evidence tiers: LITERATURE-SUPPORTED and NOVEL PREDICTION.

**KRAS G12V**
Three binding site probes are identified in the Switch II pocket region, consistent with published fragment screening data confirming Switch II pocket accessibility in G12V crystal structures (Broker et al., J Med Chem 2022). Two highly accessible surface lysines provide positive control options. Evidence tiers: LITERATURE-SUPPORTED and NOVEL PREDICTION.

**KRAS G12R**
Switch II pocket accessibility is confirmed. Published work demonstrates covalent targeting of Arg12 in the Switch II pocket (Fell et al., JACS 2022), providing independent evidence of pocket tractability. The engine identifies complementary binding site probes and surface probes. Evidence tiers: LITERATURE-SUPPORTED and NOVEL PREDICTION.

### 5. What The Dataset Delivers

Each mutant section includes:

- Ranked binding site probe recommendations with synthesis guidance
- Ranked surface probe recommendations for positive controls
- Do Not Pursue list — absent, buried, or problematic residues
- Evidence tier classification for every recommendation
- Structural data table — top 10 scored residues with SASA, proximity, and score

### 6. Limitations and Scope

- All recommendations are computational predictions and require experimental validation
- Static crystal structures do not capture protein dynamics in solution
- LYS and TYR novel prediction sites have not been experimentally confirmed as SDA crosslinking sites; experimental validation is required before drawing structural conclusions
- This dataset covers SDA photoaffinity probe design specifically; other covalent warhead chemistries are not addressed
